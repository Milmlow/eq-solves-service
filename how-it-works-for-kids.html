The Asset Helper
— How It Works! —

A friendly story about how grown-ups count thousands of electrical switches in giant computer buildings, without losing track.

🏢   ⚡   🔌   📋   💻 Big building → Lots of electricity → Many switches → A special sheet → Our helper computer
1

🏢What's a Data Centre?

A data centre is a huge building full of computers. When you watch a video, send a message, or play a game online, it zooms in and out of places like this. They're like libraries — but for the whole internet.

Inside, there are hundreds of big metal cabinets. Each cabinet has loads of switches called breakers. Breakers are like tiny lifeguards for electricity — if something goes wrong, they jump in and stop the power before anything gets hurt.

2

📋Why do we write stuff down?

Every single breaker needs a name tag. What it does, how big it is, where it lives, who made it. Why? Because if one ever has a hiccup, the team needs to find it fast — and know exactly what was there before.

There might be hundreds of breakers in one job. That's a lot of name tags! Humans are brilliant, but typing into the same form a hundred times is slow and boring. And when people are bored, they make small mistakes.

Fun fact: Some of the cabinets Royce's team works on are as tall as your dad and have more switches inside than a classroom has pencils. 🖍️
3

📝The Magic Green-Box Sheet

Our customer gives us a special spreadsheet — like a really organised worksheet. Some boxes are locked (we don't touch those). The boxes we need to fill in are painted green. Kind of like a colouring book that tells you exactly where to colour.

A technician can sit down with this sheet and fill in all the green boxes on their computer — even without internet. That's way faster than tapping through a web form.

4

🪄Teaching the Computer What They Found

When the sheet is all coloured in, the technician goes to our Asset Helper website, opens the job, and drops the file onto a page called Load capture. That's where the magic happens.

The helper looks at every green box, figures out which switch it belongs to, and shows a preview first — "I found 36 switches and 756 pieces of info." No saving yet!

If everything looks right, you click one button and all the answers save at once. Hundreds of boxes, one click. 🎉

What if the helper gets confused? If it can't find a switch, it'll show you a list so you can fix the sheet. It never guesses. That's really important for a job like this.
5

🕵️The "Who Did What" Diary

Here's the clever bit. Every answer the helper saves also writes down:

  • Who wrote it
  • When they wrote it
  • Where it came from (the web form, or a sheet with this name)

This is called an audit log — kind of like a diary for the information itself. Months later, if someone asks "who said this switch was 250 amps?", we can answer in seconds.

Think of it this way: If your homework goes missing, a diary that says "I gave it to Ms Smith on Tuesday at 3pm" is really useful. Our helper keeps that diary automatically. 📓
6

🎁Handing it Back to the Customer

When the job is done, our helper puts everything into two tidy files:

1. The customer's own spreadsheet — with all the green boxes filled in perfectly. Looks just like they sent it, but complete.

2. An audit log — the full diary, so anyone can check who did what and when.

That's the handover! A happy customer, a proud team, and a promise that every answer can be traced. ⭐

🧠 Word Wall

Data centre
A big building full of computers that run the internet.
Breaker
A small switch that turns off electricity if something goes wrong. A safety helper.
Capture
A fancy word for "writing down the answer." One capture = one answer about one switch.
Template
The special spreadsheet with green and grey boxes.
Import / Export
Import = the helper reading a file in. Export = the helper giving a file back out.
Upsert
A mashup of "update" and "insert." If an answer is new, add it. If it already exists, update it. Either way, the answer ends up correct.
Audit log
A diary that records who did what and when. Very useful if someone asks questions later!
